# Datasets

- MMED (Multimodal Micro-Expression Dataset, audio+video): Newly introduced dataset capturing synchronized audio with 
  spontaneous micro-expressions; designed specifically to study audio–visual synergy in MEs and reports 
  LOSO benchmarks, making it the best candidate for ME at fine temporal scale with audio

- K-EmoCon (naturalistic conversations): Multimodal with audiovisual and physiological signals, continuous annotations; 
  can extract short windows around subtle facial changes and run ASR for text.​

- Micro-expression recognition studies commonly combine CASME II, SAMM, and SMIC and reduce labels to 2–3 classes because of 
  imbalance and scarcity, highlighting the need to curate balance if tri-modal data is engineered via ASR

- EAV (EEG–Audio–Video): Balanced per emotion by design in the paper and includes audio and video; you could transcribe speech 
  and use the provided EEG or ignore it, yielding audio–video–text for categorical emotions

- UTeMo (audiovisual emotion recognition): Public dataset with seven basic emotions and explicitly stated balanced class counts 
  across the seven classes; provides audio and video, enabling reliable ASR to obtain text for a third modality
